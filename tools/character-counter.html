Guide to Readability Scores

Different formulas measure text complexity in different ways. For general web content, aiming for a grade level of around 8 is a good target.

  • Flesch-Kincaid Grade Level: The most common score, representing a US school-grade level. It's based on average sentence and word length.
  • Gunning Fog Index: Estimates the years of formal education needed to understand the text on the first reading. It focuses on sentence length and "complex" words (3+ syllables).
  • Coleman-Liau Index: Works by counting characters per word and sentences per 100 words, making it computationally faster than syllable-based scores.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Why are the readability scores different?

Each score uses a different formula to measure complexity. Flesch-Kincaid focuses on word and sentence length, Gunning Fog emphasizes 'complex' words (3+ syllables), and Coleman-Liau uses character counts. It's best to look at them together to get a general sense of your text's readability.

What is a good keyword density for SEO?

There is no "perfect" number, and you should always prioritize writing naturally for your audience. However, a general guideline for many SEO professionals is to aim for a keyword density of around 1-2%. This tool helps you monitor that while you write.

How does the 'Read Aloud' feature work?

The "Read Aloud" feature uses your browser's built-in Text-to-Speech (TTS) engine to vocalize the text. It's a great way to proofread, as hearing your writing read back can help you catch awkward phrasing and typos that your eyes might otherwise miss.
