About the Advanced PDF Compressor

This tool gives you maximum control over your PDF file size, all without everuploading your file. By re-processing the images inside your PDF at different quality and resolution (DPI) settings, it can achieve significantly better compression than simple metadata removal.

How It Works:

  • Image Re-compression: The main source of PDF bloat is large, high-resolution images. This tool rebuilds your PDF by rendering each page as an image and re-compressing it using high-efficiency JPEG settings.
  • DPI Control: You can choose the resolution (Dots Per Inch). 150 DPI is great for on-screen reading, while 72 DPI is even smaller. This is especially effective on scanned documents.
  • Grayscale Conversion: Removing color information is a simple and highly effective way to cut file size, often with no loss of readability for text-based documents.

How to Use This Tool

  1. Upload File: Drag and drop your PDF or click "Select File".
  2. Choose Level: Select a compression preset. "Recommended" is great for most uses.
  3. Set Options: For extra compression, check "Convert to Grayscale".
  4. Compress: Click the "Compress PDF" button and wait for the processing to finish.
  5. Download: Review your savings and click "Download" to get your new, smaller file.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Is this PDF compressor secure?

Yes, 100%. All processing happens entirely within your web browser. Your PDF file is never uploaded to any server, ensuring your data remains completely private.

How does this tool compress PDFs?

This tool compresses PDFs by re-compressing the images inside them. It reads each page, renders it at the DPI (resolution) and quality setting you choose, and then builds a new PDF from these new, smaller images. This is the most effective way to reduce file size, especially for scanned documents.

What do the compression levels mean?

They are presets for quality and resolution (DPI):
- Extreme: Uses a low 72 DPI and low image quality. Creates the smallest files, but text might be blurry.
- Recommended: A balance of 150 DPI and medium quality. Great for most documents, offering significant size reduction with good readability.
- High Quality: Uses a high 300 DPI and high image quality. File size will be larger, but it's best for documents with important photos.

What does 'Convert to Grayscale' do?

Checking this option will remove all color information from your PDF, converting it to black and white. This can dramatically reduce the file size, especially when combined with a strong compression level.

Why did my file size INCREASE after compression?

This can happen with PDFs that are text-only. An original text-based PDF is extremely small because it just stores font data. This tool compresses by converting pages into images. A full-page image (even compressed) will be larger than the original text data. This tool is designed for image-heavy files like scans and photos, where it provides huge file size savings.
