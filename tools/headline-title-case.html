What is Title Case & Why is it Important?

Title case is a specific style of capitalization used for titles of works like books, articles, movies, songs, and blog posts. Unlike sentence case (where only the first word and proper nouns are capitalized) or UPPERCASE (where everything is capitalized), title case capitalizes most words but keeps certain small words (like articles, prepositions, and conjunctions) lowercase.

Using correct title case is crucial for:

  • Professionalism: Adhering to standard capitalization rules makes your writing look polished and credible.
  • Readability: Title case helps readers quickly scan and understand headlines and titles.
  • Style Guide Compliance: Many academic and publishing style guides (like APA, MLA, Chicago) have specific rules for title case.

Manually applying these rules can be tedious and error-prone. This tool automates the process, ensuring your titles are perfectly formatted according to common conventions.

About Title Case Formatting Rules

Proper title case isn't just about capitalizing every word. Different style guides (like APA, Chicago Manual of Style, MLA, AP) have slightly different rules, especially regarding prepositions and conjunctions.

The Common Rules Our Tool Follows

This converter applies a widely accepted set of rules suitable for most headlines and titles:

  • The first and last word of the title are always capitalized.
    Example: The Beginning and the End
  • All "major" words (nouns, pronouns, verbs, adjectives, adverbs) are capitalized.
    Example: Quickly Brown Fox Jumps
  • Short coordinating conjunctions, articles, and prepositions (our "minor words" list: a, an, and, as, at, but, by, for, in, nor, of, on, or, so, the, to, up, yet) are kept lowercase unless they are the first or last word.
    Example: War and Peace; A River Runs Through It (Note: 'Through' is often capitalized in some styles, but lowercase in others if short).

Always double-check against your specific style guide requirements if submitting for publication.

Currently, this tool capitalizes the first part of a hyphenated word if it meets the criteria, but may not always capitalize subsequent parts according to specific style guide rules (e.g., "Step-by-Step" vs. "Step-By-Step"). For complex hyphenation, always refer to your style guide.
