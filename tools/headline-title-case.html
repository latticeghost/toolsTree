About Title Case Formatting

Title case is essential for professional documents, articles, books, and website headlines. Unlike simple "Capitalize Every Word," proper title case adheres to style guides (like The Chicago Manual of Style or AP Style) by ensuring small connecting words are kept in lowercase. This dedication to grammar and style is what makes your content look polished.

The Rules Our Tool Follows

Our converter is built with the most common journalistic and academic rules, specifically:

  • The first and last word of the title are always capitalized.
  • All major words (nouns, verbs, adjectives, adverbs) are capitalized.
  • Small connecting words (prepositions, articles, and conjunctions of four letters or fewer) such as a, an, the, and, but, or, for, nor, of, in, with are kept in lowercase, unless they are the first or last word.
