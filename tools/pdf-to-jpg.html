About the PDF to Image Converter

This tool allows you to convert every page of a PDF document into a separate image file, all without uploading your data. You get full control over the output format and quality, ensuring the images are perfect for your needs.

Key Features:

  • JPG vs. PNG: Choose the right format for your content. JPG is ideal for photos, while PNG provides a crystal-clear, "lossless" image perfect for text and diagrams.
  • Quality Control: Select from 300 DPI (High), 150 DPI (Medium), or 72 DPI (Low) to get the perfect balance between image quality and file size.
  • 100% Secure & Private: The entire conversion process runs in your browser. Your PDF never leaves your computer.
  • Batch Download: All your converted images are instantly bundled into a single .zip file for your convenience.

How to Use This Tool

  1. Upload File: Drag and drop your PDF or click "Select File".
  2. Choose Format: Select JPG (for photos) or PNG (for text/graphics).
  3. Choose Quality: Select High, Medium, or Low DPI. Medium is great for most uses.

  5. Download: Click "Download .zip File" to save all your images.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Is this PDF to JPG converter secure?

Yes, it is 100% secure. Your PDF file is processed entirely in your web browser. It is never uploaded to our servers, ensuring your data remains completely private.

How do I convert a PDF to JPG?

Simply upload your PDF, choose your image format (JPG or PNG) and quality, and click 'Convert'. The tool will process each page and provide a .zip file containing all your images.

What's the difference between JPG and PNG?

Use JPG for photos and complex images to get a smaller file size. Use PNG for text-heavy pages, diagrams, or line art, as it provides a much clearer, higher-quality image for that type of content (at the cost of a larger file size).

What do the quality settings mean?

They control the resolution (DPI) of the output images:
- High (300 DPI): Best for high-quality prints or detailed graphics.
- Medium (150 DPI): A great balance of quality and file size for on-screen viewing.
- Low (72 DPI): Creates the smallest images, perfect for email thumbnails or quick previews.
