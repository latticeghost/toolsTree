What is a Recurring Deposit (RD)?

A Recurring Deposit (RD) is a savings tool that allows you to invest a fixed amount every month for a predetermined period. It's offered by banks and financial institutions and earns interest, typically compounded quarterly. It's an excellent way to build savings through disciplined monthly contributions.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What does 'Compounding Frequency' mean?

This is how often the interest earned is added to your principal balance. "Quarterly" (every 3 months) is most common for RDs in India. More frequent compounding leads to slightly higher returns due to the effect of earning interest on your interest sooner.

Why is the 'Value in Today's Money' lower?

This value accounts for inflation, which is the rate at which money loses its purchasing power over time. While your final maturity amount might be ₹10 Lakhs in 10 years, its actual buying power might only be equivalent to ₹6 Lakhs today. This inflation-adjusted figure gives you a more realistic understanding of your future wealth.
