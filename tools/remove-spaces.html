Cleaning Modes Explained

  • Normalize Spaces: Replaces consecutive spaces, tabs, and line breaks with a single space.
  • Remove All Spaces: Deletes only space characters (` `), leaving tabs and line breaks intact.
  • Remove All Whitespace: The most aggressive mode. Deletes all spaces, tabs, and line breaks.
  • Remove Empty Lines: Deletes any lines that are completely empty or contain only whitespace.
  • Trim Lines: Removes leading and trailing whitespace from the beginning and end of each individual line.
  • Tabs to Spaces: Converts every tab character into a specified number of space characters.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

'Remove All Spaces' vs 'Remove All Whitespace'?

"Remove All Spaces" only deletes the literal space character. "Remove All Whitespace" is more powerful and also deletes tabs, line breaks, and other invisible whitespace characters.
