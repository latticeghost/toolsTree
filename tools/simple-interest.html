What is Simple Interest?

Simple Interest (SI) is calculated only on the original principal amount of a loan or investment. Unlike compound interest, you don't earn interest on the interest itself, resulting in linear growth.

Formula for Interest (SI)

$$ \text{SI} = \frac{\text{P} \times \text{R} \times \text{T}}{100} $$

Formula for Principal (P)

$$ \text{P} = \frac{\text{A}}{1 + (\frac{\text{R} \times \text{T}}{100})} $$

Formula for Rate (R)

$$ \text{R} = \frac{(\frac{\text{A}}{\text{P}} - 1)}{\text{T}} \times 100 $$

Formula for Time (T)

$$ \text{T} = \frac{(\frac{\text{A}}{\text{P}} - 1)}{\frac{\text{R}}{100}} $$

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What's the difference between Simple and Compound Interest?

Simple interest is calculated only on the initial principal amount. Your interest earnings do not change over time. Compound interest is calculated on the principal amount plus the accumulated interest from previous periods. This "interest on interest" effect leads to exponential growth over time and is far more common in real-world savings and investment products.

Can I use this calculator for loans?

Yes, absolutely. The principles are the same. For a loan, the "Principal" is the amount you borrowed, the "Interest" is the extra amount you pay back to the lender, and the "Total Amount" is the full sum you will repay over the loan's duration.
