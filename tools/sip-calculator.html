Understanding the SIP Formula

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) is a method of investing a fixed sum regularly, typically monthly. It leverages the power of compounding, where your returns also start earning returns. The calculator determines the Maturity Amount (M) using the formula:

$$ \text{M} = \text{P} \times \left[ \frac{(1+i)^n - 1}{i} \right] \times (1+i) $$
  • M: Maturity Amount
  • P: Monthly SIP Amount
  • i: Periodic Interest Rate (Annual Rate / 12)
  • n: Total Number of Installments (Years × 12)

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What's the difference between a SIP and a lump sum investment?

A SIP involves investing a fixed amount regularly (e.g., monthly), which helps in averaging out purchase costs over time (rupee cost averaging). A lump sum is a one-time large investment. SIPs are ideal for salaried individuals to build wealth with discipline, while lump sums can be effective if you have a large amount of money and believe the market is at a good entry point.

Is the 'Expected Annual Return' guaranteed?

No, it is not guaranteed. The return on market-linked investments like mutual funds (where SIPs are common) fluctuates based on market performance. The rate you enter should be a realistic assumption based on the fund's historical performance or your financial advisor's guidance. This calculator is a tool for estimation, not a guarantee of returns.
